Policy drafter journey map (Germany - Bund)

Based on legal frameworks and service.bund.de requirements for higher civil service (Höherer Dienst) roles.

Stages and steps Choosing career options Education and training Working as a policy drafter in Germany Change job or retire
Consider public service
Decide on a career working at the interface between politics, administration, and law-making.
Apply for Law
Enroll in a University Law Degree (Rechtswissenschaften) to prepare for "Volljurist" roles.
First State Exam
Complete university studies and pass the First State Examination (Erste juristische Prüfung).
Legal Clerkship
Complete the compulsory 2-year legal clerkship (Rechtsreferendariat) in various legal institutions.
Second State Exam
Pass the Second State Exam to become a fully qualified lawyer (Volljurist) with judicial eligibility.
Search Portals
Search official portals for "Referent" openings in the senior civil service (Höherer Dienst).
Selection Process
Apply for roles in specific government bodies and undergo the multi-stage selection process.
Onboard Referent
Begin as a probationary civil servant (Beamter auf Probe) or equivalent tariff employee drafting primary policies.
Impact Assessment
Perform regulatory impact assessments (Gesetzesfolgenabschätzung) and evaluate laws.
Legislation Focus
Manage inter-ministerial coordination, stakeholder hearings, and federal state involvement.
Continuous Training
Continuously engage with European directives, parliamentary procedures, and interdisciplinary topics.
Tenure & Promotion
Attain lifetime civil servant status (Verbeamtung auf Lebenszeit) and advance to senior management.
Rotations
Transition through rotating assignments across different departments or to EU/international bodies.
Retirement
Retire after decades of service with a senior civil service pension (Pension).
